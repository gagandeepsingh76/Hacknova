What is Virtual Reality?

Virtual Reality (VR) is an immersive computer‑generated environment that simulates presence in a digital world. Using a headset and motion tracking, VR lets you look around, move naturally, and interact with 3D spaces as if you were physically there. It is widely used in learning, simulation, engineering, medicine, architecture, remote collaboration, and entertainment.

Immersion Presence Interaction Spatial Audio 6DoF Tracking

Quick Facts

  • • Head‑mounted displays create stereoscopic 3D.
  • • Controllers or hands enable interaction.
  • • Tracking maps real movement into virtual space.
  • • Used for safe, repeatable practice of complex tasks.
